Whisky or whiskey is a type of distilled alcoholic beverage made from fermented grain mash.
Various grains (which may be malted) are used for different varieties, including barley, corn,
rye, and wheat. Whisky is typically aged in wooden casks, which are often old sherry casks or
may also be made of charred white oak.

Whisky is a strictly regulated spirit worldwide with many classes and types. The typical unifying
characteristics of the different classes and types are the fermentation of grains, distillation,
and ageing in wooden barrels.
